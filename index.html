1.1. [1954] Godzilla AKA Gojira Ishirô Honda

1.4. [1964] Ghidorah, the Three-Headed Monster AKA San daikaijû: Chikyû saidai no kessen by Ishirô Honda

    My brain was split between the seriousness of the human plot (if a little sci-fi-esque) and the hilarity of the monster fights. Overall it was quite entertaining!

1.5. [1964] Godzilla vs Mothra AKA Mosura tai Gojira by Ishirô Honda

  • A greedy developer has placed huge machines to suck dry a part of the ocean near Tokyo so he can put luxury condos there. After a storm, a giant egg washes up on the beach nearby and is immediately put on public display. The developer's plans go awry when he disrupts Godzilla's rest and the monster goes stomping through Tokyo again. It's up to the elderly Mothra, and then to its two offspring, to save Tokyo from destruction.

    Concept: 5/7. I actually liked the general plot, and I thought it was relatively well-written. The subplots were pretty good. That said, there was nothing to really follow, and I don't think I'd really recommend it based on the story.

    Execution: 3/7. I thought the pacing was slow. I almost never turn off movies once I've started them, but I have to admit that some of this felt painful to me.

    Aesthetics: 6/7. I love all the sounds, visuals, and the score of Godzilla movies that I've seen. This one was no different! Even though it's obviously dated, it still looked and sounded pretty good in 2020.

2. 80s revival/90s run

2.1. [1984] Godzilla 1985: The Legend is Reborn AKA Gojira AKA The Return of Godzilla by Kôji Hashimoto

  • From out of the South Seas comes a gigantic monster that attacks Tokyo and devastates Japan.The film, despite being preceded by other Godzilla movies, chronologically takes place after the first film, ignoring the events of the previous sequels. It was also the first in the Heisei Series of Godzilla films, despite being filmed in the Showa Era. It was Tanaka's intent to restore the darker themes and mood of the early films in the series. The film's redesign of Godzilla's facial features, giving him a darker appearance, would make the Heisei incarnation popular among fans.

  • people seem to really like this one as a "straight up" godzilla – it's a good and scary one that doesn't lean too far into the bizarre

    I really love the tone and aesthetic of the Heisei series. I'm rewatching/watching them for the first time as an adult and really impressed with how great they look and how tense and frightening they can be.

2.2. [1989] Godzilla vs. Biollante AKA Gojira tai Biorante by Various Directors

  • A scientist combines the cells of a rose with those of Godzilla to create a biological creature more horrifying than any seen before. The two do battle after a destructive tour of Japan by Godzilla. A newly released version includes many new fight scenes that were cut out of the theatrical version.

    Wow, this was a great one. The very end gets a bit silly with some rushed tying of loose ends but overall this might be my favorite godzillla movie. Biollante is horrific and beautiful, probably the most intense-looking and scary antagonist in the series. Godzilla also looking fierce like an angry tiger. Really great fantastic looking water battles.

    I'm really glad this lived up to its awesome poster.

2.3. [1991] Godzilla vs. King Ghidorah AKA Gojira vs. Kingu Gidorâ by Kazuki Ômori and Kôji Hashimoto

  • Time travelers use Godzilla in their scheme to destroy Japan to prevent the country's future economic reign.

    This entry takes a dip back into the sillier side of the series. It's still more serious than before but definitely a few steps back from the return of godzilla and godzilla vs biollante which are both intense and even scary movies.

    Charming and a lot of fun while bringing some interesting plot developments to the series. A bit of an odd blend of serious stuff and campiness. I way prefer the tone of the previous two movies. The poster had me expecting something much darker.

    Godzilla meets T2. Trying to kill Godzilla like he's John Connor. LOL

2.4. [1992] Godzilla vs Mothra: The Battle for Earth AKA Gojira vs. Mosura by Takao Okawara

  • The Earth is headed for disaster and when an archeological research team visits Infant Island to find out why, they discover two tiny women who reveal that the Earth is fighting back for all the harm humans have done here and sends out the evil Battra to destroy us. The Cosmos, as the girls are called, offer their help by calling Mothra to battle the creature. Unfortunately, Godzilla also appears and a three way battle begins that threatens to destroy Japan.

    I'm a bit torn by this movie: On the one hand, I like how close it is to the original. On the other hand, I wish they did more with the story and effects 30 years later.

2.6. [1994] Godzilla vs. SpaceGodzilla AKA Gojira VS Supesugojira by Various Directors

  • When Mothra went into outer space to stop a meteor from reaching Earth, she accidentally took cells from Godzilla and remaining cells from Biollante that inhabited our atmosphere. The combined cells went through a black hole and created a new creature, Space Godzilla. Space Godzilla heads to Earth to confront Godzilla, Junior Godzilla, and the new G-Force robot, MOGERA.

    Your typical Gojira movie, but with better cinematography and great, great design for the villain. Just beautiful. Also feels like a great time to still shoot film and miniatures. The technology is understood, the budget is there.

    If you know what a cheesefest and trainwreck plot is usually a Godzilla movie, you'll have fun. Cause Space Godzilla was awesome.

2.7. [1995] Godzilla vs. Destoroyah AKA Godzilla vs. Destroyer AKA Gojira tai Desutoroiâ by Various Directors

  • Something has destroyed Birth Island, home of Godzilla and Little Godzilla and soon, it is discovered that Godzilla has developed a bright flaming glow, indicating that his nuclear energy is growing out of control. Fearing that Godzilla will soon explode, the G-Force tries to freeze him, thus cooling his temperature. But another problem arises as a horde of human sized creatures, formed from a combination of Godzilla cells, and the weapon that destroyed the original one, The Oxygen Destroyer. Now the military must try to stop these creatures and stop Godzilla from going through a nuclear meltdown that could destroy the world.

    This is fun. Over the top soundtrack, good dub, and some awesome models.

    Thought this one was pretty awesome! Destroyer is a cool opponent, I like the nods to Aliens as well. After watching this I can confidently say that the Heisei-era Godzilla films are my favorites, even though the Millennium series has a few good'ns as well (plus an awesome character design).

This time he battles a mysterious UFO that later transforms into a mysterious kaiju dubbed Orga. They meet up for the final showdown in the city of Shinjuku.
